    Research Library¶

    Evidence sources that support Terroir whitepaper claims and architecture decisions.

    Georgian Wine¶

    Record revenues from wine and spirits exports¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2025
    • Cited in: Abstract, Executive Summary, Overview, Rollout, Conclusion
    • Source: https://wine.gov.ge/En/News/38181
    • APA: National Wine Agency of Georgia. (2025, January 20). Record revenues from wine and spirits exports. https://wine.gov.ge/En/News/38181

    Summary: Official 2024 export results for Georgian wine and spirits, including volume, revenue, and destination-country growth.

    Why it matters: Provides the export-exposure baseline used to justify why counterfeit risk and verification matter at border and distributor level.

    The 8,000-year-old uninterrupted tradition of wine production in Georgia¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2023
    • Cited in: Overview
    • Source: https://wine.gov.ge/En/News/37074
    • APA: National Wine Agency of Georgia. (2023, June 6). The 8,000-year-old uninterrupted tradition of wine production in Georgia. https://wine.gov.ge/En/News/37074

    Summary: National Wine Agency background on Georgia's long viticulture history and its large base of indigenous grape varieties.

    Why it matters: Supports the paper's cultural and biodiversity rationale for starting the provenance pilot in Georgia.

    Traditional Georgian winemaking method in qvevri¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2013
    • Cited in: Overview
    • Source: https://ich.unesco.org/en/decisions/8.COM/8.13
    • APA: UNESCO. (2013). Decision of the Intergovernmental Committee: Traditional Georgian winemaking method in qvevri. https://ich.unesco.org/en/decisions/8.COM/8.13

    Summary: UNESCO recognition of qvevri winemaking as an element of intangible cultural heritage.

    Why it matters: Anchors the whitepaper's claim that Georgian wine identity is not only commercial but also cultural and institutional.

    Annual report 2020¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2020
    • Cited in: Overview, Trace, Appendix: Formula Notes and Implementation Glossary
    • Source: https://wine.gov.ge/En/Files/Download/12996
    • APA: National Wine Agency of Georgia. (2020). Annual report 2020. https://wine.gov.ge/En/Files/Download/12996

    Summary: National Wine Agency report covering vintage accounting, quality-control reforms, and lab methods used to detect falsification.

    Why it matters: Provides direct evidence that Terroir can align with existing transparency and lab-control workflows instead of inventing a parallel bureaucracy.

    Anti-Counterfeit and GI¶

    Counterfeit wine labeled with appellation of origin 'Kindzmarauli' revealed in Russia¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2018
    • Cited in: Abstract, Problem, Overview, Trace, Threats
    • Source: https://www.sakpatenti.gov.ge/en/news/5309/
    • APA: National Intellectual Property Center of Georgia (Sakpatenti). (2018, August 9). Counterfeit wine labeled with appellation of origin 'Kindzmarauli' revealed in Russia. https://www.sakpatenti.gov.ge/en/news/5309/

    Summary: Cross-border enforcement case involving counterfeit bottles using a protected Georgian appellation.

    Why it matters: Shows that Georgian wine counterfeiting is documented and enforceable, not a hypothetical future risk.

    Counterfeit Georgian wine batch removed from sale in Ukraine¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2018
    • Cited in: Problem
    • Source: https://wine.gov.ge/En/News/25735
    • APA: National Wine Agency of Georgia. (2018, December 31). Counterfeit Georgian wine batch removed from sale in Ukraine. https://wine.gov.ge/En/News/25735

    Summary: Official notice that counterfeit Georgian wine was identified and removed from the Ukrainian market.

    Why it matters: Supports the paper's argument that verification has to work across jurisdictions and import markets.

    Law of Georgia on appellations of origin and geographical indications of goods¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 1999
    • Cited in: Overview, Trace, Governance
    • Source: https://www.wipo.int/wipolex/en/text/217274
    • APA: World Intellectual Property Organization. (1999). Law of Georgia on appellations of origin and geographical indications of goods. WIPO Lex. https://www.wipo.int/wipolex/en/text/217274

    Summary: The Georgian legal framework governing appellations of origin and geographical indications.

    Why it matters: Establishes that Terroir complements an existing GI regime rather than replacing it with a technical system.

    Georgia strengthens the protection of its geographical indications¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2025
    • Cited in: Governance
    • Source: https://www.wipo.int/web/wipo-magazine/articles/georgia-strengthens-the-protection-of-its-geographical-indications-67406
    • APA: World Intellectual Property Organization. (2025, February 25). Georgia strengthens the protection of its geographical indications. https://www.wipo.int/web/wipo-magazine/articles/georgia-strengthens-the-protection-of-its-geographical-indications-67406

    Summary: WIPO coverage of Georgia's continuing international GI protection posture.

    Why it matters: Shows institutional momentum around origin protection and strengthens the case for digital enforcement accelerators.

    Global trade in fakes: A worrying threat¶

    • Type: report
    • Year: 2022
    • Cited in: Problem, Overview
    • Source: https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/global-trade-in-fakes-a-worrying-threat_74c81154-en.html
    • APA: Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development. (2022). Global trade in fakes: A worrying threat. OECD Publishing. https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/global-trade-in-fakes-a-worrying-threat_74c81154-en.html

    Summary: OECD synthesis of the scale, channels, and enforcement implications of counterfeit trade.

    Why it matters: Provides the macro-level trade context for why authenticity infrastructure has real economic value.

    Mapping global trade in fakes 2025: Global trends and enforcement challenges¶

    • Type: report
    • Year: 2025
    • Cited in: Problem, Threats
    • Source: https://doi.org/10.1787/94d3b29f-en
    • APA: Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development, & European Union Intellectual Property Office. (2025). Mapping global trade in fakes 2025: Global trends and enforcement challenges. OECD Publishing. https://doi.org/10.1787/94d3b29f-en

    Summary: Joint OECD and EUIPO report on counterfeit flows, market trends, and enforcement conditions.

    Why it matters: Supports the whitepaper's claim that counterfeit risk is structurally cross-border and increasingly sophisticated.

    • Cited in: Problem, Shield

    Agroinsurance¶

    • Type: official
    • Year: 2025
    • Cited in: Overview, Shield, Rollout
    • Source: https://www.rda.gov.ge/en/projects/Agroinsurance
    • APA: Rural Development Agency. (2025). Agroinsurance. https://www.rda.gov.ge/en/projects/Agroinsurance

    Summary: Program page describing Georgia's subsidized agricultural insurance framework, including coverage areas and co-financing.

    Why it matters: Shows that Georgia already has a policy and operational substrate for risk-transfer collaboration, making Shield more realistic as a next module.

    Index insurance for agricultural resilience: Promises, challenges and solutions¶

    • Type: report
    • Year: 2022
    • Cited in: Abstract, Executive Summary, Problem, Overview, Shield, Conclusion, Appendix: Formula Notes and Implementation Glossary
    • Source: https://openknowledge.worldbank.org/entities/publication/1be2d7c8-9b22-5753-bf07-4a0df44bc09d
    • APA: World Bank. (2022). Index insurance for agricultural resilience: Promises, challenges and solutions. https://openknowledge.worldbank.org/entities/publication/1be2d7c8-9b22-5753-bf07-4a0df44bc09d

    Summary: World Bank synthesis on why index-based and parametric mechanisms help, where they fail, and how basis risk should be treated.

    Why it matters: Provides the conceptual backbone for the Shield trigger, payout, and basis-risk sections.

    Is index insurance a viable solution for disaster risk financing in agriculture?¶

    • Type: report
    • Year: 2019
    • Cited in: Executive Summary, Shield, Appendix: Formula Notes and Implementation Glossary
    • Source: https://openknowledge.worldbank.org/entities/publication/e6cc66f4-a4b3-5355-bded-ef17f4f9d4b3
    • APA: World Bank. (2019). Is index insurance a viable solution for disaster risk financing in agriculture? https://openknowledge.worldbank.org/entities/publication/e6cc66f4-a4b3-5355-bded-ef17f4f9d4b3

    Summary: World Bank note on operational design, index quality, and implementation limits of agricultural index insurance.

    Why it matters: Supports the decision to frame Shield first as an auditable relief and resilience layer rather than a fully regulated insurance product.

    Climate resilience pathways for European wine regions¶

    • Type: paper
    • Year: 2024
    • Cited in: Problem, Shield
    • Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s43016-024-00988-w
    • APA: Moriondo, M., Ferrise, R., Dibari, C., and colleagues. (2024). Climate resilience pathways for European wine regions. Nature Food, 5, 551-562. https://www.nature.com/articles/s43016-024-00988-w

    Summary: Peer-reviewed assessment of climate risk and adaptation pathways across wine-producing regions.

    Why it matters: Gives scientific grounding to the whitepaper's claim that resilience data belongs alongside provenance data in vineyard-adjacent infrastructure.

    Blockchain technology for sustainable supply chain management¶

    • Type: paper
    • Year: 2021
    • Cited in: Executive Summary, Architecture, Appendix: Formula Notes and Implementation Glossary
    • Source: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tifs.2021.08.012
    • APA: Paliwal, V., Chandra, S., & Sharma, S. (2021). Blockchain technology for sustainable supply chain management: A systematic literature review and a classification framework. Trends in Food Science & Technology, 109, 637-660. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tifs.2021.08.012

    Do farmers participate in traceability systems? A systematic review and future directions¶

    • Type: paper
    • Year: 2025
    • Cited in: Abstract, Executive Summary, Overview, Trace, Threats, Conclusion, Appendix: Formula Notes and Implementation Glossary
    • Source: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/joss.70034
    • APA: Banstola, A., Ghimeray, A. K., & Ahmad, T. (2025). Do farmers participate in traceability systems? A systematic review and future directions. Journal of the Science of Food and Agriculture, 105(4), 2036-2049. https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/joss.70034

    Summary: Systematic review focused on farmer adoption incentives, frictions, and governance conditions in traceability systems.

    Why it matters: Supports the paper's operational requirement that Trace remain batch-first, low-friction, and aligned to existing workflows.
